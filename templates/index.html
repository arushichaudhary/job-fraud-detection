Good to know

A few questions, answered

What does "fraudulent" mean here?

It means the listing shares strong patterns with known fake job postings: vague duties, requests for money, or urgent pressure. Treat it as a signal, not a guarantee, and still use your own judgement.

What should I fill in?

As many fields as you can. The description and requirements give the model the most to work with, so don't leave those short if you can avoid it.

Is this a substitute for my own research?

No, think of it as a second opinion. Still look the company up separately, and never pay anyone to "secure" a job offer.

What happens after I submit?

The details are run through the classifier and a sealed verdict opens right away, marked either verified or flagged.
